## 1. Overview

AI-powered SEO content production is not about generating more content faster.
It is about using AI to remove the mechanical parts of content work — outlines,
first drafts, metadata, semantic gap analysis, so that humans can focus on the
parts AI cannot replicate original research, first-hand experience, genuine
expertise, and editorial judgment.

Across the 10 experts researched, there is strong consensus on one principle:
**AI as a support layer, not a replacement for strategy.**

This playbook takes clear positions on all three.

---

## 2. Step by step procedure for SEO‑First Content SOP 

This SOP is designed for teams producing AI‑assisted SEO content in 2026. It focuses on **user‑centric topics, topical authority, and real‑world expertise**, not just rankings.

---

## Step 1 — Strategic content selection (user need + product)

Before writing, decide what to create based on **user intent and product value**, not keyword volume alone.

### Key checks
- Is there a genuine user demand for this topic (search intent, not just volume)?  
- Does finding this page move the user closer to your product or solution?  
- Can you cover this better—more specific, credible, or original—than current top‑ranking pages?

If any answer is unclear, research first (GSC, user interviews) before drafting.

**Source:**  
https://www.shortform.com/summary/product-led-seo-summary-eli-schwartz

---

## Step 2 — Customer‑first topic and keyword research

Start with **real‑world customer language**, then use tools to validate, not to invent ideas.

### Actions
- Talk to Sales, Customer Success, and Support: “What questions keep coming up that our content doesn’t answer?”  
- Export Google Search Console data and focus on queries on pages 2–4 of results.  
- Use tools (Ahrefs/Semrush) only to validate volume and competition, not to generate topics.  
- Prioritize long‑tail, highly specific queries that match real user problems.  

Then group topics into clusters (pillar + 4–8 supporting pages) for a topical map.

**Source:**  
https://majestic.com/seo-in-2025/aleyda-solis

---

## Step 3 — Build a topical map, not just one page

Google rewards sites that cover a subject **comprehensively**, not just one “hero” article.

### How to build the map
- Define your core topic (e.g., “AI‑driven SEO tools for SaaS”).  
- Use AI (Claude/ChatGPT) to list subtopics and common questions, then manually group them into clusters.  
- Score each cluster by:
  - Search demand  
  - Business relevance  
  - Your credibility to cover it well  
Only build clusters that score high on all three.

This map becomes your content backlog and refresh roadmap.

**Source:**  
https://www.marketingspeak.com/a-new-era-in-seo-with-cyrus-shepard/

---

## Step 4 — AI‑assisted drafting with real‑world substance

AI is an assistant, not a full‑service writer; the **workflow** matters more than the tool.

### Workflow
1. Write the outline manually. Structure sends the strongest SEO signal.  
2. Load reference material into your AI workspace:
   - Your 5–10 best posts  
   - Top‑ranking competitor articles  
   - Any original data or case studies  
3. Prompt section‑by‑section, not “write a full article in one go.”  
4. Inject at least one of each per piece:
   - Original data or statistic  
   - Real‑world example or case study  
   - Customer quote (if available)  
   - Named, credentialed author with a visible bio  
5. Edit ruthlessly for clarity and tone, and remove generic AI phrasing.

**Source:**  
https://www.pageoptimizer.pro/blog-author-kyle-roof

---

## Step 5 — On‑page SEO, refreshes, and systems

Optimize, then build a system around **continuous refinement**, not “publish and pray.”

### On‑page SEO
- Place the target keyword and semantically related terms in:
  - Title tag  
  - H1 and first 100 words  
  - At least one H2  
  - Meta description  
  - Image alt text  
- Use tools like PageOptimizer Pro (or similar) that score pages based on 400+ controlled tests; aim for 80+ and add surfaced terms.  
- Maintain proper header hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3), internal links, and schema (Article, FAQ, How‑To) where relevant.

### Refreshes and systems
- Prioritize refreshing underperforming pages (weak CTR or slipping rankings) over new content, especially for sites with 50+ posts.  
- Maintain a 30–90‑day content calendar with clear roles:
  - AI drafter  
  - Human editor  
  - SEO optimizer  
- Run weekly ranking reviews and a 6‑month refresh cycle for all pieces older than 6 months.

**Source:**  
https://www.seoforgooglenews.com/p/google-march-2024-core-update

## 4. Where Experts Disagree

### Disagreement 1 — Algorithmic On-Page Math vs. Human Expertise Signals

**Kyle Roof** argues SEO is fundamentally mathematical. His controlled tests
show that pages can rank even with placeholder content when on-page
mathematical signals are correct. The algorithm responds to structure and
keyword patterns, not semantic meaning alone.
(Source: Kyle Roof, HVSEO blog, 24.07.2024,
hvseo.co/blog/demystifying-seo-insights-from-high-voltages-kyle-roof/)

**Lily Ray and Cyrus Shepard** argue that post-2023, Google's algorithm has
shifted heavily toward user experience and Quality Rater signals. Algorithmic
exploits are increasingly short-term — each major update has specifically
targeted mathematical optimization without genuine content value.
(Sources: Lily Ray, ipullrank.com/seo-week-2025-lily-ray, 25.08.2025;
Cyrus Shepard, marketingspeak.com/a-new-era-in-seo-with-cyrus-shepard/,
25.02.2025)

"I think Google pulled a big magic trick with us by calling it a helpful content update. I don’t think it had anything to do with content. When you look at the winning and losing sites, it has to do with the aggressiveness of SEO. It was really helpful content. I just call it an anti-SEO update because that seems to be what it was, which hurts me because I’m part of a group of people who taught folks for years how to do better in Google’s algorithm. And all of a sudden, boom, those tactics stop working like they used to. And it’s been a big readjustment for me." - shepard

**My take: Side with Ray/Shepard—algorithmic exploits are short‑term

Both layers are required, and skipping either is a mistake.**
In low-competition niches, Roof's math is sufficient — you can win without
strong brand signals. In competitive spaces where top-ranking pages all have
strong on-page signals, the differentiator shifts to E-E-A-T and user
experience signals. Use POP to hit the baseline, then compete on expertise.
These are sequential steps, not competing philosophies.

---

### Disagreement 2 — End-to-End AI Automation vs. Human-Guided Strategy

**Matt Diggity** has built and published a no-code AI system that automates
content production and link signals with minimal human involvement, arguing
speed and system efficiency are the competitive advantage.

(Source: Matt Diggity, LinkedIn, 28.10.2025,
linkedin.com/posts/mattdiggityseo_i-built-a-no-code-ai-seo-system-that-with-activity-7389287329569529856-JJcn)
https://youtu.be/xG9Vu9yrQzw?si=N6UwlzdLciFvMicr



**Aleyda Solis** argue human-guided strategy is
non-negotiable. Solis warns against applying any framework without semantic
intent alignment to each specific topic cluster — a form of lazy automation
that produces technically complete but undifferentiated content.

(Sources: Cyrus Shepard, marketingspeak.com, 25.02.2025; Aleyda Solis,
advancedwebranking.com/blog/adapting-old-seo-rules-to-the-new-ai-search,
13.07.2025)

"What I do instead is take the information I obtain from those tools to identify patterns more easily, to prioritize what matters most in my own context—rather than starting the whole thing from scratch using an LLM." - aleyda


**My take: Automate production, never automate strategy.**
Diggity's system works for affiliate and e-commerce sites where content is
largely informational and brand trust is a weaker buying signal. Automated content without editorial judgment is a brand liability.
Use AI to scale drafting. Keep humans in control of what gets covered, how it
is framed, and what goes live.

---

### Disagreement 3 — Keyword Research Tools vs. Customer Research as Starting Point

**Brendan Hufford** Keywords no longer lead; start with customer problems.

(Source: Brendan Hufford,
marketingpowerups.com/brendan-hufford-content-ip-framework/, 11.05.2023)

"Brendan Hufford's Content IP framework is based on the idea that the old way of generating demand through keyword research is no longer effective. Instead, marketers should focus on creating intellectual property around the problems their customers have" - Brendan Hufford

**Neil Patel** argue keyword tools remain essential for
validating demand and measuring competition. Patel's NP Digital data across
385,749 content pieces shows keyword-targeted blog articles drive 29% of
organic traffic.
(Sources:Neil Patel, YouTube, youtube.com/watch?v=tZjuJtV44bI, 11.06.2025) 2:01- Evalo your keyword strategy 

**My take: Hufford generates the ideas; keyword tools validate and prioritize them.**
These are sequential, not competing. Customer and sales conversations generate
topics grounded in real pain. Keyword tools tell you which of those topics has
measurable search demand and how hard they are to rank for. Skipping the
customer step produces generic keyword-matched content. Skipping the keyword
step produces content nobody is searching for.

## 5. What I Rejected and Why

### Rejected Idea 1 — Fully Automated Publishing Pipelines

Some practitioners advocate AI pipelines that write, optimize, and publish content with zero human review.

Why I rejected it: 
Lily Ray traces Google's March 2024 core update — which reduced "unhelpful content" by 45% directly to this approach. Sites built on automated AI publishing were hit hardest.

Automated pipelines cannot produce original data, first-hand experience, or genuine expertise. 

(Source: Lily Ray, ipullrank.com/seo-week-2025-lily-ray, 25.08.2025)


---

### Rejected Idea 2 — SEO Measured Only by Rankings and Traffic

Several sources treat SEO as a channel measured by rankings and organic
clicks, with an implicit assumption that traffic eventually leads to revenue.

**Why I rejected it:** Eli Schwartz's Product-Led SEO framework is explicit:
SEO measured only by rankings and traffic becomes a cost center with no
defensible business case. Kevin Indig independently argues that as AI
Overviews reduce click-through rates for informational queries, click volume
is an increasingly unreliable proxy for business value.

Without tying content production to specific conversion metrics — trial
signups, demo requests, MQL volume — it is impossible to know whether your
AI SEO program is creating business value or just filling a content calendar.

(Sources: Eli Schwartz, elischwartz.co/book;
Kevin Indig, LinkedIn, 27.06.2023,
linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_seo-content-growth-activity-7079930737474342912-c6RJ)

## 6. My Original Ideas

### Idea 1 — AI‑SEO Content‑Maturity Radar

Concept:

Create a simple checklist that every AI‑assisted content piece must pass before publishing:

Has human value
Original research, case studies, interviews, or real‑world data, not just rewritten sources.
Inspired by Cyrus Shepard and Lily Ray on “experience and effort” signals.
Ties to your brand
Clear author, product, and expertise (who you are, who you help, and why you’re qualified).
Aligned with Aleyda Solis and Neil Patel on brand‑centric content. 
Ready for AI search
Clear structure, direct answers, and headings that both users and AI‑search layers can easily parse.
Reflects Aleyda Solis and Eli Schwartz on AI‑search‑ready UX. 
Moves the user forward
Helps the visitor move closer to a trial, signup, or purchase, not just “answer a question.”
Fits Kevin Indig and Eli Schwartz’s product‑led SEO thinking. 
Each piece gets a quick score (1–5) on these four points. If the average is below ~3.5, it either gets upgraded or doesn’t get mass‑published.

## Weaknesses of This Playbook

1. **Assumes a human editor is available** — Small teams or
   solo founders may not have bandwidth for deep human review
   on every piece. The playbook needs a lighter version for
   resource-constrained teams.

2. **Tool recommendations may become outdated quickly** — AI
   SEO tools are evolving fast. Surfer SEO and PageOptimizer
   Pro recommendations may be outdated within 6-12 months.

3. **No data from actual B2B SaaS case studies** — This
   playbook is built from expert opinions and frameworks, not
   from tested B2B SaaS campaigns. Results may vary.

4. **Google algorithm changes are unpredictable** — Any SEO
   playbook carries the risk that Google updates its algorithm
   and changes what works overnight.

5. **Assumes existing domain authority** — Some steps like
   topical authority building assume the site already has some
   domain authority. New sites may need a different approach.

## Who I Would NOT Recommend Following

### Neil Patel

I would not recommend Neil Patel as a primary source for
B2B SaaS SEO specifically, for these reasons:

1. **Scale mismatch** — Neil Patel operates at massive scale
   (enterprise level with large teams). His advice often
   assumes resources that most B2B SaaS companies do not have.

2. **Volume over depth** — His content strategy favors
   publishing high volumes of content quickly. This works for
   media sites and consumer brands, but B2B SaaS buyers expect
   depth and expertise — not volume.

3. **Generic advice** — Much of his recent YouTube content
   repeats the same high-level frameworks without the specific,
   testable tactics that experts like Kyle Roof or Cyrus Shepard
   provide.

**I still recommend consuming his content** for big-picture
trends and marketing direction — but not as your primary
tactical guide for B2B SaaS SEO.
